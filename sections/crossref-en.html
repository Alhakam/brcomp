Capc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/brcomp#Cap

Non-traveled edge formation of reinforced concrete or prestressed concrete bridges, which can also be formed as kerbs. caps have to fulfil various functions. In addition to protecting the load-bearing bridge structure, they are used for anchoring passive protective devices and as bicycle and/or pedestrian paths. Due to their exposed position, they are exposed to particularly strong attacks, e.g. frost-thaw salt attacks. In addition, there are dynamic loads caused by traffic on the bridges. Even if the bridge caps undergo optical changes as a result of wear, load or weathering during the planned service life, concrete bridge caps are safe and durable if constructive and technical rules are observed.
has super-classes

Construction jointc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/brcomp#ConstructionJoint

Separation lines within components. They occur, for example, in concrete construction as set-down joints between successive concreting sections. Concreting in several stages is often unavoidable due to the construction process. The aim is to create the best possible bond between the adjacent concreting sections. Since the reinforcement runs through the construction joints, a traditional shuttering of the concreting section with wood would be costly. Today, expanded metal is mostly used for this purpose, which can be easily cut and bent to size and guarantees a certain degree of interlocking of the concreting sections. If, for example, a straight line of joints is desired for aesthetic reasons, formwork panels can be used in combination with a reverse bending connection for the reinforcing irons, which allow subsequent interlocking by bending up the reinforcing bars as the concreting process continues. In engineering structures such as bridges, screw connections are used to connect the reinforcement steel over construction joints due to the higher loads.
has super-classes
joint c

IRI: https://w3id.org/brcomp#RockerBearing

Fixed bearing that only allows tilting movements around the bearing axis (one-sided tilting). They are only suitable if no significant tilting movements are to be expected across the bridge axis; i.e. for straight or slightly curved bridges with small bearing spacings as well as rigid cross members and rigid piers.
has super-classes

Roller bearingc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/brcomp#RollerBearing

One-sided movable Bearings which allow one-sided tilting. They have a limited use like the line tilting bearings. One or more cylindrical rollers are located between the plane-parallel surfaces of the upper and lower bearing plates. In the standard version with one roller, tilting around the roller axis is possible. It is possible to transmit horizontal forces perpendicular to the direction of movement. Since the direction of movement must be perpendicular to the tilting direction, they are not suitable for inclined bridges.

Bearing that transmits vertical forces and allows movements in at least one direction. c
